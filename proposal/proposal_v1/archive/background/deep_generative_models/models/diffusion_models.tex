\paragraph{Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models.}
Most recently, Diffusion Models \citep{pmlr-v37-sohl-dickstein15, NEURIPS2020_4c5bcfec} and Score-Based Generative Models \citep{sgm_yang_song_2023} have achieved state-of-the-art results. Diffusion models define a forward noising process
\begin{equation}
q(x_t \mid x_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}(x_t; \sqrt{1-\beta_t}\, x_{t-1}, \beta_t I),
\end{equation}
where $\beta_t$ controls the noise variance schedule. Score-based models instead estimate the score function
\begin{equation}
s_\theta(x) = \nabla_x \log p_{\text{data}}(x),
\end{equation}
and use Langevin dynamics
\begin{equation}
x_{k+1} = x_k + \frac{\epsilon}{2} s_\theta(x_k) + \sqrt{\epsilon}\, \eta_k, \quad \eta_k \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I).
\end{equation}
